\section{Introduction}
This document describes the database schema for the \textbf{Svasthya Fresh Backend Application}.
The schema supports authentication, inventory management, coupon handling, order processing,
payment integration, and audit features such as price history tracking.

% ================= USER =================

\section{User Management Tables}

\subsection{users}
\begin{longtable}{|p{4cm}|p{9cm}|}
\hline
id & Primary key \\ \hline
name & User full name \\ \hline
email & Unique email \\ \hline
mobile & Unique mobile number \\ \hline
password\_hash & Encrypted password \\ \hline
role & ADMIN / CUSTOMER \\ \hline
is\_active & Account status \\ \hline
created\_at & Creation timestamp \\ \hline
updated\_at & Update timestamp \\ \hline
\end{longtable}

\subsection{addresses}
\begin{longtable}{|p{4cm}|p{9cm}|}
\hline
id & Primary key \\ \hline
user\_id & FK referencing users \\ \hline
building\_no & Building number \\ \hline
building\_name & Building name \\ \hline
street\_no & Street number \\ \hline
area\_name & Area / locality \\ \hline
city & City \\ \hline
state & State \\ \hline
pincode & Postal code \\ \hline
is\_default & Default address \\ \hlineaddress_type & Address type (home/office/other) \\ \hlinecreated\_at & Creation timestamp \\ \hline
\end{longtable}

\section{Conclusion}
This schema follows normalization principles, ensures secure authentication,
supports password recovery flows, variant-level inventory, and price auditability,
while maintaining clean separation between orders and payments.
